## 2. EU Safety Regulations

### 2.1 Caffeine Limits

#### Per Serving Limits
- **Maximum caffeine per serving**: 80mg
- **Recommended range**: 50-80mg per serving
- **Minimum effective dose**: 30mg per serving
- **Maximum concentration**: 150mg per 100ml

#### Daily Intake Limits
- **Maximum daily caffeine**: 400mg for healthy adults
- **Pregnant women**: Maximum 200mg daily
- **Sensitive individuals**: Consider lower limits
- **Children**: NO caffeine recommended (application requires 18+)

#### Calculation Examples
```
Safe Limits Example:
- 250ml serving: 80mg caffeine maximum
- 500ml serving: 160mg caffeine maximum  
- 1000ml serving: 320mg caffeine maximum
- Daily total: Never exceed 400mg
```

### 2.2 EU Food Additive Regulations

#### Approved Additives
- **Taurine**: No specific limit, generally recognized as safe
- **Citric acid**: No specific limit for beverages
- **Sodium citrate**: Approved for food use

#### Banned Substances
- **Titanium Dioxide (E171)**: BANNED in EU since August 2022
- **Artificial colors**: Restricted to approved types only
- **Sweeteners**: Only EU-approved varieties
- **Preservatives**: Limited to food-grade concentrations

### 2.3 Labeling Requirements

#### Mandatory Information
- **Caffeine content**: Must be clearly stated

## 4. Ingredient Safety

### 4.1 Caffeine Anhydrous

#### Lethal Dose Information
- **LD50 (oral, rat)**: 192mg/kg body weight
- **Human lethal dose estimated**: 3-5 grams
- **Dangerous dose**: 1-2 grams can cause severe toxicity
- **Warning dose**: 200-400mg can cause adverse effects

#### Handling Precautions
- **Weigh in ventilated area**: Avoid inhaling fine particles
- **Use proper containers**: Airtight, labeled containers
- **Avoid skin contact**: Can cause irritation
- **Store locked away**: Away from children and pets
- **Label clearly**: "HIGH CAFFEINE - DANGEROUS"

#### Symptoms of Overdose
- **Mild (200-400mg)**: Rapid heartbeat, anxiety, tremor
- **Moderate (400-800mg)**: Nausea, confusion, dizziness
- **Severe (800mg+)**: Hallucinations, seizures, cardiac arrhythmia
- **Lethal (3000mg+)**: Respiratory failure, cardiac arrest

### 4.2 Taurine

#### Safety Profile
- **Generally recognized as safe** by FDA and EFSA
- **No specific toxicity limits** established
- **Well-tolerated** in typical energy drink amounts
- **Natural amino acid** found in the body

#### Safe Levels
- **Energy drinks**: 1000-2000mg per serving typical
- **Upper limits**: No established toxic level
- **Side effects**: Rare, usually mild (headache, nausea)

### 4.3 Flavor Concentrates

#### Chemical Hazards
- **Concentrated chemicals**: Much stronger than food versions
- **Skin/eye irritation**: Can cause chemical burns
- **Respiratory hazards**: Vapors can be irritating
- **Flammability**: Some concentrates are flammable

#### Handling Safety
- **Use minimal amounts**: Start with less than recommended
- **Good ventilation**: Work in well-ventilated areas
- **Protective equipment**: Gloves and goggles required
- **Clean spills immediately**: Follow chemical spill procedures

### 4.4 Preservatives

#### Sodium Benzoate (E211)
- **Maximum level**: 0.1% in beverages
- **Safety profile**: Generally safe at approved levels
- **Concerns**: Can form benzene with vitamin C under certain conditions
- **Handling**: Avoid inhalation, avoid contact with eyes

#### Potassium Sorbate (E202)
- **Maximum level**: 0.3% in beverages
- **Safety profile**: Generally recognized as safe
- **Handling**: Minimal hazard, standard food handling precautions

## 7. Emergency Procedures

### 7.1 Caffeine Overdose Response

#### Recognition of Symptoms
- **Mild symptoms**: Rapid heartbeat, anxiety, tremor, nausea
- **Moderate symptoms**: Confusion, dizziness, vomiting, chest pain
- **Severe symptoms**: Hallucinations, seizures, difficulty breathing
- **Critical symptoms**: Cardiac arrest, respiratory failure

#### Immediate Actions
1. **Stop consumption immediately**
2. **Call emergency services** (112 in Netherlands)
3. **Provide information**: Amount consumed, time, symptoms
4. **Monitor vital signs**: Heart rate, breathing, consciousness
5. **Do not induce vomiting** unless instructed by medical professionals

#### Medical Information to Provide
- **Amount of caffeine consumed** (in mg)
- **Time of consumption**
- **Age and weight** of person
- **Current symptoms**
- **Any medications** being taken
- **Medical history** (heart conditions, etc.)

### 7.2 Chemical Exposure Response

#### Skin Contact
1. **Remove contaminated clothing** immediately
2. **Flush with copious water** for at least 15 minutes
3. **Seek medical attention** for chemical burns
4. **Bring product information** to medical facility

#### Eye Contact
1. **Flush immediately** with clean water for 15 minutes
2. **Remove contact lenses** if easily removable
3. **Seek immediate medical attention**
4. **Do not rub eyes**

#### Inhalation
1. **Move to fresh air** immediately
2. **Loosen tight clothing**
3. **Monitor breathing** and provide rescue breathing if needed
4. **Seek medical attention** for respiratory symptoms

### 7.3 Spill Response

#### Small Spills (<100ml)
1. **Contain spill**: Use absorbent materials
2. **Ventilate area**: Ensure good air circulation
3. **Clean thoroughly**: Use appropriate cleaning methods
4. **Dispose properly**: Follow local waste disposal regulations

#### Large Spills (>100ml)
1. **Evacuate area**: Ensure safety of all personnel
2. **Call hazardous materials team** if needed
3. **Contain spill**: Prevent spread to drains or waterways
4. **Document incident**: Photos and written report

## 8. Age and Health Restrictions

### 8.1 Age Requirements

#### Minimum Age
- **18+ years**: Application requires 18+ verification
- **Legal age for caffeine**: Varies by country, typically 16-18+
- **Parental consent**: Not acceptable for minors
- **Age verification**: Required for all users

#### Health Restrictions

#### Absolute Contraindications
- **Heart conditions**: Arrhythmias, coronary artery disease
- **Pregnancy**: Especially first trimester
- **Breastfeeding**: Caffeine passes to infant
- **Anxiety disorders**: Can exacerbate symptoms
- **Sleep disorders**: Can worsen insomnia
- **High blood pressure**: Uncontrolled hypertension

#### Relative Contraindications
- **Mild heart conditions**: Requires medical supervision
- **Diabetes**: Monitor blood sugar carefully
- **Medication interactions**: Check with healthcare provider
- **Substance abuse history**: Increased risk of dependency

### 8.2 Health Monitoring

#### Before Starting
- **Medical consultation**: Recommended for health conditions
- **Baseline assessment**: Heart rate, blood pressure
- **Medication review**: Check for interactions
- **Allergy assessment**: Check for ingredient allergies

#### Ongoing Monitoring
- **Regular health checkups**: Especially cardiovascular health
- **Symptom tracking**: Keep diary of adverse effects
- **Tolerance assessment**: Monitor changing tolerance levels
- **Dose adjustment**: Modify consumption based on health status
